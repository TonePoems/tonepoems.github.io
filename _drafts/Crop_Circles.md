# Crop Circles

I can get [nerd sniped]() by some pretty stupid stuff. This one originally came from a thought at work that I couldn't stop thinking about so I did some quick research to scratch that itch and be able to focus again. Below are the rough calculations, hopefully with some more citations and math this time.

Epistemic status: Low

The landscape around my brother in Colorado is truly breathtaking. The ring of mountains around the San Louis Valley gives a good view in any direction, while the immediate valley is flat enough to look across the entire distance*. Looking at it from an airplane or Google Maps, you will notice lots of large circles across the ground. I have seen these in other places, but never been so intrigued by them until now. 

![Picture of Center Pivot Irigation fields]()

These are called center pivot irrigation. 

They look way too much like crop tiles in a city builder or other similar game. Something with the message *Produces 20 wheat a second*. Well, now I HAVE to know how much they would produce in such a game. Enter some back of a napkin math, that has (thus far) not been accepted by any scientific journals.
